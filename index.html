Winnipeg Jets Home


Bell MTS Place is the home of the Winnipeg Jets.

The Winnipeg Jets are a professional ice hockey team based in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. They are members of the Central Division of the Western Conference of the National Hockey League (NHL). The team is owned by True North Sports & Entertainment and plays its home games at Bell MTS Place.


Fans gather at Portage and Main after the announcement.

The current rendition of the Jets began play as the Atlanta Thrashers in the 1999-2000 NHL season. However in May 2011 True North Sports & Entertainment then bought the team and relocated the franchise from Atlanta, Georgia to Winnipeg prior to the 2011-12 season (the first NHL franchise to relocate since the Hartford Whalers became the Carolina Hurricanes in 1997). The team was renamed the Jets after Winnipeg's original WHA/NHL team, which relocated after the 1995-96 season to become the team now known as the Arizona Coyotes.
